# Agent Marketplace — CUSTOMERS

Two-sided. Both sides bootstrap from the existing platform (#1/#3) audience.

## Supply side (creators)
- **C-1: Indie devs / agencies** who built agents/MCP servers (often via #3) and want reach + revenue.
- **C-2: SaaS companies** publishing official MCP servers/agents for their product (distribution into AI workflows).
- **C-3: Power users in ContextOS (#1)** who built useful internal agents/workflows worth sharing.

## Demand side (consumers)
- **D-1: ContextOS teams (#1)** wanting pre-built capabilities installed one-click into workflows. (Primary — built-in distribution.)
- **D-2: Eng teams** seeking trusted agents/servers vs. building from scratch.
- **D-3: Enterprises** wanting curated, security-reviewed, private/org marketplaces.

## Anti-personas
Anyone pre-platform (no distribution = cold start); low-trust/unsafe listings; non-technical buyers.

## Buyer vs. user
Consumers buy (usage/subscription); creators earn (revenue share); platform takes a cut. Distribution = the ContextOS (#1) user base (why we build last).

## Prioritization
Bootstrap supply from #3 generators + #1 power users; bootstrap demand from #1 teams; then open public marketplace; then enterprise private registries.

## Validation
- (Creators) Would you publish + monetize your agent/server here? At what revenue share?
- (Consumers) Would you install a security-reviewed agent into your workflow? What trust signals do you need?
