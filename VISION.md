# Agent Marketplace — VISION

## North Star
> Become the **ecosystem** for AI agents, MCP servers, and workflows — where the best capabilities are discovered, trusted, installed, and monetized.

## Picture
As agents/MCP proliferate, teams won't build everything; they'll want trusted, pre-built capabilities — and creators will want reach + revenue. The vision: the curated, secure, monetized marketplace that connects them, deeply integrated with ContextOS (#1) so install = one click into real workflows. The network effect (more supply → more demand → more supply) becomes the lab's durable moat.

## Why last
Marketplaces die from cold-start without distribution + trust. We earn those first: #1 (distribution: a user base installing into real workflows), #3 (supply: generated servers), #4 (trust: observability). Only then does a marketplace thrive. Building it Year 3 is a *sequencing* decision, not a hedge.

## Phases
| Horizon | What |
|---------|------|
| Y3 H1 | Listings + discovery + one-click install into #1 + ratings |
| Y3 H2 | Security review + revenue share + workflows |
| Y4+ | Private/enterprise registries, certification, partner ecosystem |

## Principles
1. **Trust first:** security-reviewed, rated, observable (via #4) listings.
2. **One-click install into real workflows** (#1 integration) — utility over a catalog.
3. **Aligned economics:** usage-based revenue share.
4. **Curated, not a dumping ground** (avoid the GPT-Store quality problem).
5. **Build only after distribution exists.**

## The bet
That the platform (#1–#4) earns enough distribution + trust to overcome marketplace cold-start, and that owning the agent/MCP/workflow ecosystem — with curation, security, and revenue share — is the lab's most durable, network-effect moat. High risk, high reward; correctly sequenced last.
